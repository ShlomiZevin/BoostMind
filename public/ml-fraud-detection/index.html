Pitfalls I know in my gut

Where fraud systems fail — and how I prevent it

The 4 classic risks I see in every fraud detection project — and how I put a safety net under each from day 1.

🎭

Label Leakage

A feature that updates after the decision (e.g. "chargeback_count") — the model shows 99% accuracy in backtest and falls apart in production. My first check: rigorous temporal validation, point-in-time correctness.

⚖️

Class Imbalance

Your fraud rate is probably 0.1%-2%. A "lazy" model that always says "legit" — 99% accurate. I work with SMOTE, class weights, focal loss, and measure PR-AUC, not ROC-AUC.

🌊

Concept Drift

Fraud tactics shift monthly. A static model decays in 3-6 months. I build monitoring that detects decay before it shows up in business numbers.

💸

False Positives Out of Control

If FP rate gets too high, analysts lose trust and rubber-stamp everything — "alert fatigue". I track analyst review rate and set thresholds that preserve reasonable precision.
