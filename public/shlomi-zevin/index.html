The shift

Travel ads are losing. Micro-drama is winning.

Short videos built as ongoing storylines — same characters, evolving situations, each clip pulling the audience into the next — have become the strongest format in social marketing right now. They don't get scrolled past. They get followed.

For a hotel, this is a fundamentally different value than a one-off sponsored post. Your property doesn't appear once and disappear. It becomes a location inside a story the audience is actively returning to — watched, shared, saved, and quoted to a friend.

Hotels that step into this format now get exposure that builds and compounds across weeks, not days.

What I do

Everyday-life comedy that hits because it's true.

I create nuanced sketches built around characters everyone instantly recognizes from their own life: the family member, the colleague at work, the cashier at the grocery store, the type you meet everywhere.

Each character takes serious time to develop — building their profile, writing their scripts, dialing in their look with filters until they feel completely real. The most common comment I get on every video is "how is this so true?". People connect because they see themselves and their world reflected back at them.

The proposal

The story I'd build at your hotel.

Take my recurring characters and place them inside the hotel — into the room, by the pool, at breakfast, the bar, the lobby. Each clip stands on its own, but also makes the audience want to see the next. The property becomes the natural backdrop of an ongoing series.

What I'm offering

  • 1–3 short comedy sketches per day during the stay, each shot at your hotel — naturally featuring the room, pool, restaurant, breakfast, views, and atmosphere as part of the story.
  • Your hotel mentioned and tagged in every sketch.
  • Additional sketches published over the following weeks from on-site footage — extending the exposure well beyond the stay itself.

What this requires

  • A 3–4 night hosted stay for 2 people. That's it. No fees, no media buy.

For hotels in particular, the format scales naturally. The visit is just the production window — the content keeps performing for weeks afterward.
